So far in heat transfer, we've covered conduction, convection, radiation, energy balances, resistances, fins, the lumped capacitance method, etc. all of which are useful when designing future devices. To practice these skills, homeworks are assigned; however, out of all of these assignments, I believe that homework 4 helped me the most with my understanding of the current topics that we are learning. Specifically, it touches upon all of the skills and topics aforementioned (besides lumped capacitance), which allows us to use old skills and apply them to the new ones that we are learning when completing the homework. Getting familiarized with resistances and energy balances will help build a solid foundation for future topics and can be useful for future instances, like needing to find the temperature at a device's surface or inner components. Additionally, the homework introduced what fins are and the efficiency of fins at dissipating heat, which generates eagerness to apply this design concept to future and current projects. As such, although the answers in the pdf below may not be 100% correct, the process of completing the homework allowed me to more thoroughly understand the topics.
